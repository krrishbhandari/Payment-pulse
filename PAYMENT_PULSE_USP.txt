PAYMENT PULSE SCORE VS. CIBIL SCORE
---------------------------------
Traditional CIBIL scores are designed for banks and lenders. Payment Pulse is designed for businesses and recovery agents.

| Feature             | CIBIL / Traditional Credit Score         | PAYMENT PULSE "ADVANCED RISK SCORE"                 |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| **Primary Focus**   | Credit History, Loan Mix, Hard Inquiries | Outstanding Behavior, Recency, Debt Severity      |
| **Timing**          | Backward-looking (Historical)            | Forward-looking (Predictive)                      |
| **Updates**         | Monthly or Quarterly                     | Real-Time (On every transaction/status change)    |
| **Context**         | General Creditworthiness                 | B2B Invoice & Account Specific Risk               |
| **Severity Logic**  | Binary (Late vs. On-time)                | Weighted (Amount × Days Overdue × Pattern)        |
| **Trend Tracking**  | Static Score                             | Dynamic Trend Analysis (Improving/Worsening)      |
| **Actionability**   | "Approve/Reject" Decision                | "Call/Email/Legally Escalate" Strategy            |

*The Payment Pulse Score uses a sophisticated 4-factor breakdown:*
1.  Days Overdue (40% Weight) - Immediate severity
2.  Outstanding Amount (30% Weight) - Financial exposure
3.  Payment Behavior (20% Weight) - Habitual vs. situational analysis
4.  Recency (10% Weight) - Engagement tracking
